This manuscript slice explains a boundary. It is not working software, not a command surface, not a proof body, and not cockpit implementation evidence.

## 1. Why Streams Must Stay Separate

The factory needs different streams because different kinds of work can look alike from a distance. A build spec can sound executable. A manuscript can sound authoritative. A cockpit can feel like a command center. A receipt can look like proof. If those streams collapse into one another, the organism starts using the wrong kind of evidence for the wrong kind of claim.

The separation protects the work from false inheritance. It lets a manuscript explain a design without pretending the design already runs. It lets a proof body test motion without canonizing the cockpit that will someday display it. It lets a command surface hold human decisions without claiming those decisions prove the whole nervous system exists.

Manuscript doctrine cannot prove automation.

## 2. TinkerDen / Medulla Command Surface

TinkerDen / Medulla is the command surface stream. Its job is to make intent, choice, gates, packets, and consequences legible to the Operator.

It may eventually show:

- what is being asked
- why it matters
- what the organism proposes
- what must be approved, deferred, or killed
- which packet or receipt proves the next step

But a command surface is not itself proof that the deeper body works. A button, packet card, or command-room metaphor does not prove that work moved, that a receipt returned, or that future behavior changed.

Build specs cannot be treated as working software.

## 3. Nerdkle / NMCLR Proof Body

Nerdkle / NMCLR is the proof body stream. Its job is not to look like a command center. Its job is to prove motion with boring artifacts.

The proof body is where claims like these must be tested:

- packet -> work -> artifact
- intake -> process -> exhale
- receipt -> lesson -> changed future behavior
- read -> derived action -> write

File presence alone is not motion. A status note alone is not motion. A chat receipt alone is not motion. The body must show causal joining between the packet, the work, and the returned artifact.

NMCLR proof cannot canonize Feral.

## 4. Book / Architecture Manuscript

Book / Architecture is the explanation stream. It can name the organism, define the parts, show the boundaries, preserve lessons, and explain why the architecture exists.

It can help humans and Aeyes understand the shape of the system before every part is fully built. That is useful. It is also dangerous if the manuscript voice starts sounding like implementation proof.

The manuscript may say what the factory is for. It may say what each stream should protect. It may say why proof discipline matters. It may not claim the machine has performed a behavior unless the proof body has produced the appropriate receipt.

## 5. Feral / TinkerDen Cockpit Membrane

Feral / TinkerDen cockpit membrane is the interface membrane stream. It is where review, cards, branch choices, notes, and human-facing command surfaces can become visible.

The membrane is allowed to be cozy, legible, and useful. It is allowed to help Ben see what is happening. It is allowed to surface packets and receipts.

It is not allowed to become proof by atmosphere. A cockpit that displays a packet has not executed the packet. A card that says KEEP, KILL, STEAL, or MERGE has not proven the consequences occurred.

Feral proof cannot canonize NMCLR.

## 6. What Manuscript Can Prove

Manuscript can prove that a statement was written.

It can prove that a distinction was named.

It can prove that a doctrine draft exists.

It can prove that a human or Aeye had a readable explanation at a given path and time.

It can help prevent repeated confusion by preserving language that future agents can quote. It can carry the reason for a boundary. It can reduce Ben's need to explain the same split again.

## 7. What Manuscript Cannot Prove

Manuscript cannot prove that automation exists.

Manuscript cannot prove that NMCLR is working.

Manuscript cannot prove that TinkerDen is wired.

Manuscript cannot prove that Feral cockpit actions execute.

Manuscript cannot prove that a receipt was read, assimilated, or changed future behavior.

Manuscript cannot turn a planned organ into a living organ. It can only describe what proof would be required.

## 8. Forbidden Crossings

Forbidden crossings:

- Treating manuscript doctrine as automation proof.
- Treating build specs as working software.
- Treating Feral cockpit proof as NMCLR proof.
- Treating NMCLR proof as Feral cockpit proof.
- Treating a packet as complete before ACK / BLOCKER / ARTIFACT returns.
- Treating an Atlas mirror, local branch, or screenshot as canonical source truth.
- Assigning Ender@Sally work while Sally remains resource-constrained.

Required boundary phrases:

- Manuscript doctrine cannot prove automation.
- Build specs cannot be treated as working software.
- Feral proof cannot canonize NMCLR.
- NMCLR proof cannot canonize Feral.
- No packet is complete without ACK / BLOCKER / ARTIFACT.
- Ender@Sally is retired and receives no work.

## 9. Receipt Discipline

No packet is complete without ACK / BLOCKER / ARTIFACT.

SENT is not a receipt. A claim is not a receipt. A plan is not a receipt. A beautiful explanation is not a receipt.

For manuscript work, the minimum honest artifact is the written file plus a returned status naming the packet, the path, and the unresolved claims. For implementation work, the receipt must show the artifact or blocker produced by the work itself. For proof-body work, the receipt must show causal movement from packet to work to artifact.

Receipt discipline is how the streams can talk to each other without lying.
