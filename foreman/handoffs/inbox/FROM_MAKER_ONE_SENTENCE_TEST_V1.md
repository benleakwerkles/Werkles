# FROM MAKER - ONE SENTENCE TEST V1

Execution context: `CURSOR_CLOUD_CONTAINER`.

Status: language handoff. No production deploy, SQL, secrets, billing, merge, app code change, or live data mutation is approved by this file.

## Mission

Question:

```text
What is the shortest sentence that accurately describes Werkles to a stranger?
```

Constraints:

- no internal vocabulary
- no Speaker
- no Layer 0
- no Need Translation
- no Human Opportunity Discovery
- understandable within 10 seconds

## Recommended Sentence

```text
Werkles helps people find and evaluate possible business partners.
```

## Why This Wins

It is short.

It uses plain words.

It says what the stranger gets:

```text
find possible business partners
evaluate possible business partners
```

It does not claim:

- guaranteed matches
- investment returns
- brokerage
- lending
- legal advice
- hiring authority
- verified safety
- marketplace liquidity

It fits the current product thesis:

```text
Werkles helps users browse possible fit, understand why a match appears, request an intro, and take conversations off-platform with their own advisers.
```

## 10-Second Test

A stranger should understand:

```text
Oh, it helps me figure out who might be a useful business partner and whether they are worth talking to.
```

If they ask one follow-up, it should be:

```text
What kind of partners?
```

not:

```text
What does that mean?
```

## Words Deliberately Avoided

Avoided because they are internal or too early:

- Speaker
- Layer 0
- Need Translation
- Human Opportunity Discovery
- concierge
- Forge file
- Blueprint
- Crucible
- SoleDash
- proof posture

Avoided because they create legal/commercial risk:

- investment
- deal
- broker
- lender
- guaranteed
- vetted
- verified partner
- trusted match

## Rejected Alternatives

### Too internal

```text
Werkles turns Layer 0 into Human Opportunity Discovery.
```

Fails because no stranger knows what this means.

### Too vague

```text
Werkles helps builders find what they need next.
```

Fails because "what they need next" could mean anything.

### Too marketplace-heavy

```text
Werkles is a marketplace for business partners.
```

Fails because it overstates current liquidity and implies a transactional marketplace.

### Too trust-heavy

```text
Werkles verifies business partners before you meet them.
```

Fails because verification is not the whole product and must not imply guaranteed safety.

### Too guru-ish

```text
Werkles helps founders unlock their next opportunity.
```

Fails because it sounds like generic startup fog.

### Too long

```text
Werkles helps serious builders, operators, backers, connectors, and sparks understand what they need, evaluate possible fit, and decide who to talk to next.
```

Accurate, but not a 10-second stranger sentence.

## Backup Sentence

If "evaluate" feels too dry:

```text
Werkles helps people find possible business partners and understand who is worth talking to.
```

This is warmer but longer.

## Maker Recommendation

Use this as the default one-sentence answer:

```text
Werkles helps people find and evaluate possible business partners.
```

Use the backup only when the audience needs slightly more warmth:

```text
Werkles helps people find possible business partners and understand who is worth talking to.
```
