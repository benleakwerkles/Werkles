## Doctrine

Spark is the loneliest act. The photograph must carry solitude without sentimentality. **Hopeful reality:** subject alone but frame not heavy — light, gesture, thought-in-progress carry optimism.

**Pass/fail (global):** No eye contact with camera. Attention on work. Real clothing, real room, no styling.

---

## C01 — The kitchen table

**Scene.** Pre-dawn or early evening. Small kitchen or dining table. Founder alone on something specific — notebook with marked-up pages, laptop with single spreadsheet, printed document being annotated. Household at edges: coffee mug, child's drawing on fridge, chair pushed back, bowl in sink.

**Why.** Universal side-bet symbol — household exists, work smuggled into margins. Most archetypal Spark moment.

**Pass/fail.** Cover the work with thumb — photo must still read Spark via household-at-edges. If it becomes generic kitchen portrait, household details failed.

**ID:** `spark-c01-kitchen-table`  
**Placement:** Hero primary

### Render prompt

```
Documentary photograph of a person alone at a small kitchen or dining table in early morning or late evening light, working on something specific. A notebook is open with visible handwritten marks and crossed-out lines, or a laptop is open to a single spreadsheet or document. The subject is mid-thought — pen mid-mark, finger pausing on a screen, eyes on the work. The household is visible at the edges of the frame: a coffee mug, a child's drawing on the fridge in the background, a chair pushed back, a bowl in the sink. The subject is not looking at the camera. Single source of natural light — window light at dawn, or a single lamp at night. Real clothing, real room, no styling. Composition from a three-quarter angle, slightly distant — the subject is small enough in the frame that the household around them reads. Warm realism, hopeful reality — quiet, focused, not melancholy.
```

## C02 — Before the day begins

**Scene.** Subject at actual workplace, alone, before opening or shift. Nurse in treatment room, light just on. Baker with cold ovens. Electrician at open van back sorting tools. Space is theirs — quiet, not yet in operation.

**Why.** Advanced Spark — already operating; private moment before the day claims them. *I am doing this and I see what it could still become.*

**Pass/fail.** Workspace mid-life — not new (→ Foundry), not abandoned (→ Space). History and future both visible.

**ID:** `spark-c02-before-the-day`  
**Placement:** Hero alternate · Spark gallery

### Render prompt

```
Documentary photograph of a working professional alone in their workplace before the day begins. A nurse practitioner in a treatment room with the overhead light just turned on, looking at a clipboard or scheduling screen. A baker in a small kitchen with ovens cold, looking at a notebook or a tray that hasn't been used yet. An electrician at the open back of a work van in early morning light, going through tools or consulting a list. The subject is alone, focused on something specific. They are not looking at the camera. The space is clearly working — real equipment, signs of recent and upcoming use, no styling — but it is quiet, not yet in operation. Natural light: window light, the first overhead lamp, dawn through an open door. Real clothing for the work. Composition: medium shot, the subject in the foreground, the space they work in clearly visible behind. Warm realism, hopeful reality — the quiet of a room with potential still in it.
```

## C03 — On the move with the idea

**Scene.** Transit — quiet bus/train off-peak, parked car engine off, small public bench. Portable work: notebook, phone with list, printed page. Alone but out in the world.

**Why.** Exterior Spark — idea carried between home and work; third-place thinking.

**Pass/fail.** Subject **carrying** work, not displaying. Small and private — notebook on knee, phone held low. World real and slightly unaware of subject.

**Budget cut:** Lose C03 before any other Spark shot.

**ID:** `spark-c03-in-transit`  
**Placement:** Gallery rotation only — not hero

### Render prompt

```
Documentary photograph of a person alone in transit or in a small public space, working privately on a portable idea. A subject on a quiet bus or train seat in off-peak light, a small notebook open on their knee with visible handwritten marks. A subject in a parked car with the engine off, a phone held low, a printed page on the passenger seat with pen marks. A subject on a small public bench, a folded page in one hand and a pen in the other. The subject is not looking at the camera and is not aware of being photographed. The work is small and carried — not theatrical. The world around the subject is real and slightly anonymous — a real transit interior, a real street, a real bench. Natural light, slightly warm. Composition: medium-close, the subject in the foreground, the surrounding environment soft but readable. Warm realism, hopeful reality — the private moment of an idea being carried through the world.
```
