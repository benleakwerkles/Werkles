## Doctrine

Most conceptually ambitious act — most likely to misexecute. **No people required.** One detail must say last person just left or first person hasn't arrived. Not abandonment; not real-estate aspiration.

**Pass/fail (global):** Identify sign of inhabitation. Without it → abandonment. Too clean/styled → aspiration.

---

## D01 — The space before opening

**Scene.** Working commercial space at quietest hour — early morning before staff or last light after closing. Clean, ordered, empty. Bakery prep with trays stacked. Treatment room chair toward door. Workshop tools on pegs, half-completed project on bench.

**Why.** Canonical Act II. Work paused, not abandoned. Apron on hook, light left on, cup on counter, door slightly open.

**ID:** `space-d01-before-opening`  
**Placement:** `#space` primary — replaces CSS placeholder

### Render prompt

```
Documentary photograph of a small working commercial space at the quietest hour — early morning before staff arrive, or last light after closing. No people in frame. The space is clean and ordered, but clearly in active use: a bakery prep counter with trays stacked, a small clinic treatment room with the chair angled toward the door, a workshop with tools hanging on pegs and a half-completed project on the bench. At least one specific sign of recent or imminent inhabitation is visible: an apron on a hook, a coffee cup on the counter, a single light left on, a door slightly open, a clipboard with a pen resting on it. The space has the texture of real use — slight wear, real materials, no styling. Natural light: window light, single overhead lamp in otherwise dim space, dawn through a doorway. Composition: medium-wide, the room as much as any single object, with a strong vanishing point or strong directional light. Warm realism, hopeful reality — a room waiting to be filled.
```

## D02 — The half-built space

**Scene.** Commercial space mid-build. Drywall up, fixtures not installed. Bones visible — counter location, lighting runs, front door opening. Materials staged: tile pallet, lumber, conduit.

**Why.** Space becoming — possibility more aggressive than D01; nothing happened yet but future vivid.

**Pass/fail.** Visible work-in-progress — dust, materials, uneven partial completion. Finished shell → Foundry. Demolition → abandonment.

**Rhyme:** Pairs with Forge A03 (same room type, two people added).

**ID:** `space-d02-half-built`  
**Placement:** `#space` alternate · scroll narrative pair with Forge A03

### Render prompt

```
Documentary photograph of a small commercial space mid-construction. Drywall up but unpainted, fixtures not yet installed, the bones of the future business visible: where the counter will go, where the lighting will hang, where the front door will be. Construction materials are staged in the space — a pallet of tile, a stack of lumber, a coil of electrical conduit, a sawhorse with a tape measure on it. No people in frame, but the work-in-progress is clearly active: a ladder, a partially completed section of wall, dust on the floor, a power tool resting on a sawhorse. Late-afternoon natural light coming through unfinished windows or open doorways, slightly warm. The composition reads the room as a whole — a wide-to-medium shot with strong architectural lines pulling the eye toward what the space is becoming. Warm realism, hopeful reality — a space in the act of becoming itself.
```

## D03 — Single tool at rest (tight)

**Scene.** Tight detail: wrench on bench, apron folded beside notebook, reception desk with open appointment book, van back at dawn with one tool out of slot.

**Why.** Intimate register wide shots can't provide — working life in one object.

**Pass/fail.** Object placed by use, not stylist — imperfection of real use required.

**Priority:** Lower for v1 — complement to D01/D02, not standalone load-bearer.

**ID:** `space-d03-tool-at-rest`  
**Placement:** Gallery / `#space` detail crop optional

### Render prompt

```
Tight documentary photograph of a single object at rest in a working environment, with no people in frame. A wrench laid down on a workbench next to a partially completed project. A single apron folded on a prep counter beside a small notebook with handwritten notes. A reception desk with a single open appointment book, a pen resting in the gutter, a half-empty coffee cup. The back of a work van at dawn with tools racked but untouched, one tool slightly out of its slot as if just used. The object should look placed by use, not by styling — angled with intention, surrounded by the texture of real work. Soft natural light: window light, dawn light, the warmth of a single overhead lamp. Composition: close-to-medium, the object dominant in the frame, the working environment readable around it. Warm realism, hopeful reality — the quiet weight of an object that belongs to someone's working life.
```
