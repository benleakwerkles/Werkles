## Global posture rules (all acts)

### Spark

- One person, one dream, one problem.
- Hopeful reality — alone but frame not heavy; light, gesture, evidence of thought-in-progress.
- **No eye contact with camera.** Attention on the work.
- Not melancholy, theatrical, or staged.

### Space

- **No people required.** Future exists before the team.
- Inhabited, not abandoned — one sign of recent or imminent inhabitation.
- Not real-estate listing aspiration; not demolition/abandonment.

### Forge

- Same posture rules as Spark: present, not posed; no eye contact; no presenting gesture; attention on the work.

### Foundry

- **Founder may appear**, but **work remains focal point**.
- Founder present, not posed — same posture rules as Forge.
- Reads as: *the maker is still in the frame, but the work has earned its own gravity.*

---

## Spark → Space transition (intentional rhyme)

Spark C02 (*before the day begins*) and Space D01 (*space before opening*) share the **quiet-hour register**. First frame has a person; second removes them and lets the room speak. Visitor eye carries absence as continuity.

Space D02 (*half-built*) rhymes with Forge A03 (*founder + builder in half-built space*): empty becoming-room first, then two people who make it real — conversion arc without copy explaining Werkles.
